Day 1: Prompt Engineering Mastery

Transforming vague queries into expert-level prompts

The Challenge

Using the prompt framework (ROLE ‚Üí TASK ‚Üí CONTEXT ‚Üí FORMAT ‚Üí CONSTRAINTS), transform a bad prompt into an expert-level prompt for SOC 2 audit preparation.

Before & After Comparison

‚ùå BAD Prompt

"Explain a cybersecurity risk and controls for SOC 2"

Issues: Too vague, no context, no role definition, no format specified

‚úÖ EXPERT Prompt

You are acting as a junior GRC analyst supporting a SaaS company preparing for its first SOC 2 audit. Your task is to explain a cybersecurity risk related to unauthorized access to customer data and recommend basic controls. The company stores customer data in cloud-based systems and wants to communicate risks clearly to non-technical leadership. Format: 1. Risk description 2. Business impact 3. Example controls Constraints: ‚Ä¢ Simple, non-technical language ‚Ä¢ Avoid framework jargon ‚Ä¢ Keep under 200 words

Key Learning

Structured prompts with clear role, context, format, and constraints produce exponentially better outputs. The framework transforms AI from a search engine into a domain expert.

Self-Assessment Score: 4/5

Good prompt engineering with room for refinement in constraint specificity.
